We are. We made it official quietly, and now we want the real celebration with the people we love. Same commitment, bigger party.
Do I need a car?
Not for Saturday. We're running free optional shuttles between downtown Tucson and Agua Linda Farm both ways. If you'd rather drive yourself, that's fine too. For the Friday and Sunday events around Tucson, a car or rideshare is handy but not essential if you're staying central.
What should I wear?
It scales by day: Friday welcome party is "some effort," Saturday is "more effort," and Sunday brunch is "minimal effort." For Saturday, think garden-party-meets-desert. It's partly outdoors on a working farm, so heels on grass are tough and November evenings get cool; bring a layer.
Where should I stay?
We're recommending the Barrio Viejo neighborhood in downtown Tucson, where we'll all be based and where the shuttles run from. See the Travel & Stay page for hotel options.
What's the weather like in November?
Usually excellent — daytime highs around 65–75°F, evenings dropping into the 40s–50s. Bring a layer for after sundown.
